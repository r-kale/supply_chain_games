Why did this happen? Nobody was incompetent — the system did it.

The bullwhip effect is the amplification of demand variability as orders move upstream. Its causes are structural and behavioral:

  • Demand signal processing — every tier forecasts from the orders it sees, not real demand, so each tier over-reacts to the tier below's over-reaction.
  • Lead times — with a 3-week delay between ordering and receiving, this week's panic order arrives long after the panic is over.
  • Supply-line neglect — under pressure, people forget what they've already ordered and order again. It's the single best-documented behavioral cause (Sterman, 1989).
  • Shortage gaming & batching — in real chains, rationing fears and batch ordering amplify it further.

What actually fixes it

  • Share point-of-sale demand across every tier (the retailer's demand data would have saved everyone here).
  • Cut lead times — the shorter the loop, the smaller the whip.
  • Count your supply line — inventory on order is inventory; a base-stock policy does this automatically. Compare the bot benchmark above.
  • Stabilize pricing and shrink batches so orders track true demand.
